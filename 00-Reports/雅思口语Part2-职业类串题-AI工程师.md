## 📌 话题1：Describe your perfect job

### 结构主干

| 要素 | 内容 |
|------|------|
| **What it is** | AI Engineer — developing intelligent systems and applications |
| **Where you heard about it** | Tech news, YouTube, friends in the industry |
| **What you need to learn** | Programming (Python), machine learning, math, continuous learning |
| **Why perfect** | Remote work freedom, meaningful work, can live close to nature |

### 🎤 完整口语稿（约2分钟）

> For this topic, I'd like to talk about my dream job — becoming an AI engineer.
>
> **What it is:** An AI engineer is someone who develops intelligent systems. Basically, I would create programs that can learn, make decisions, and help solve real-world problems. It could be anything from chatbots to self-driving cars to medical diagnosis tools.
>
> **Where I heard about it:** I first got interested in this field through tech news and YouTube videos. I saw how AI was changing industries, and I was fascinated. Later, I talked to some friends who work in this area, and they confirmed it's an exciting career path.
>
> **What I need to learn:** Well, there's a lot to learn. First, programming languages like Python. Then, machine learning algorithms — how to train computers to learn from data. Math is also important — statistics, linear algebra, things like that. But the thing is, this field changes so fast that you have to keep learning. It's not like you study once and you're done.
>
> **Why it's my perfect job:** This might sound unusual, but one big reason is the freedom. Many AI engineers can work remotely, which means I could live in a quieter place — maybe near mountains or forests — while still doing meaningful work. My grandfather was a farmer, and he lived close to nature every day. I want that kind of lifestyle too, but through modern technology.
>
> Also, the work itself is meaningful. You're building tools that can actually help people — diagnosing diseases, translating languages, making life easier. It's not just about making money; it's about creating something useful.
>
> So yeah, that's why being an AI engineer is my perfect job — it combines interesting work, continuous learning, and the freedom to design my own lifestyle.

## 📌 话题2：Describe one of your grandparents' job

### 结构主干

| 要素 | 内容 |
|------|------|
| **What it was** | Farmer — grew rice and vegetables |
| **How long** | Over 40 years, from young adulthood until retirement |
| **How he/she got it** | Family tradition, inherited the land and skills |
| **Skills needed** | Patience, weather knowledge, physical strength, understanding of nature |
| **Would I want this job** | No, but I respect it — I want the lifestyle, not the labor |

### 🎤 完整口语稿（约2分钟）

> For this topic, I want to talk about my grandfather's job — he was a farmer.
>
> **What it was:** He grew rice and vegetables on a small piece of land in the countryside. It wasn't a huge farm, but it was enough to support the family. He would wake up before sunrise, work in the fields all day, and come home when the sun went down.
>
> **How long he had this job:** He did this for over 40 years. He started helping his father when he was just a teenager, and then took over the farm as an adult. He only stopped when his body couldn't handle the physical labor anymore.
>
> **How he got it:** In his time, it was common to follow your family's path. His father was a farmer, his grandfather was a farmer — so he just inherited the land and the skills. There wasn't really a concept of "choosing a career" back then.
>
> **Skills this job needs:** Farming looks simple, but it requires a lot of knowledge. You need to understand the weather, the soil, the seasons. You need patience — plants don't grow overnight. And of course, physical strength. My grandfather was strong, but the work still wore him down over the years.
>
> **Would I want this job:** Honestly, no. It's incredibly hard work, and the income is unpredictable. But there's something I really admire about it — the connection to nature. He worked outdoors every day, breathing fresh air, watching things grow. That's the lifestyle I want, but through different means.
>
> That's actually why I want to become an AI engineer — so I can work remotely and live in a quieter place, close to nature, like he did. But instead of physical labor, I'll use my mind and technology.

## 📌 话题3：Describe an area/subject of science you are interested in

### 结构主干

| 要素 | 内容 |
|------|------|
| **Which area** | Artificial Intelligence, specifically Machine Learning |
| **When/where you came to know it** | High school/university, online courses, tech news |
| **How you get information** | Online courses, YouTube, research papers, coding practice |
| **Why interested** | Solves real problems, rapidly evolving, empowers my career goal |

### 🎤 完整口语稿（约2分钟）

> For this topic, I'd like to talk about Artificial Intelligence, specifically Machine Learning — which is the science behind making computers learn from data.
>
> **When I came to know it:** I first heard about AI in high school, but I didn't really understand it. Later, I saw news about AI programs beating humans in games like chess and Go, and chatbots like ChatGPT. That made me curious. I started watching videos and taking online courses to learn more.
>
> **How I get information:** Mostly through the internet. There are excellent online courses on platforms like Coursera and YouTube. I also read articles and sometimes research papers. And I practice by writing code — there are many free tools where you can experiment with machine learning models.
>
> **Why I'm interested:** There are several reasons. First, it solves real problems. AI is already being used to diagnose diseases, translate languages, drive cars, and help farmers grow better crops. It's not just theory — it's practical.
>
> Second, it's constantly evolving. What was impossible five years ago is now normal. Being part of this field means you never stop learning, which I find exciting.
>
> And finally, it connects to my career goal. I want to become an AI engineer, and understanding the science behind it is the foundation. Plus, it gives me the skills to create tools that can help people — maybe even help people like my grandfather, who worked so hard on the farm. Imagine using AI to predict weather, optimize crop growth, or reduce physical labor. That would be meaningful.
>
> So yeah, AI and machine learning is the subject I'm most interested in — it's the future, and I want to be part of building it.
