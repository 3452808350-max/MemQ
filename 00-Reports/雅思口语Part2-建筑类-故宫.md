# 雅思口语 Part 2 - 建筑类素材：故宫

---

## 📌 题目：Describe an interesting building

### 结构主干

| 要素 | 内容 |
|------|------|
| **Where it is** | Center of Beijing, known as the Forbidden City |
| **What it looks like** | Red walls, golden roofs, curved corners, moat, courtyards, stone lions, 9,000+ rooms |
| **What function it has** | Former: emperor's palace (24 emperors, 500 years) → Now: museum, UNESCO site |
| **Why interesting** | Central axis, perfect symmetry, no nails in wood structures, forbidden → open to all |

---

## 🎤 完整口语稿（约2分钟）

> For this topic, I'd like to talk about one of the most fascinating buildings in China — the Palace Museum, which most people know as the Forbidden City.
>
> **Where it is:** It's located right in the center of Beijing. You literally can't miss it — it's huge and sits on the city's central axis.
>
> **What it looks like:** Well, the first thing that strikes you is the color — big red walls with golden roofs shining in the sun. The buildings have these traditional curved corners that look like they're reaching for the sky. There's a moat surrounding the whole place, massive courtyards inside, and stone lions guarding every gate. The scale is incredible — it has over 9,000 rooms!
>
> **What function it has:** Historically, it was the emperor's home and office. Twenty-four emperors lived there over 500 years — they ate, slept, worked, and ruled the entire country from within those walls. Now, it's been transformed into a museum. People from all over the world come to see ancient artifacts, paintings, and imperial treasures. It's also recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
>
> **Why I think it's interesting:** Two things make it truly special.
>
> First, the axis. There's a perfectly straight line running through the entire complex, and all the important buildings sit on this line.
>
> Second, the symmetry. The left and right sides are almost perfect mirrors of each other. This reflects the Chinese philosophy of balance and harmony. The emperor was considered the center of the universe, so everything balanced around him.
>
> Also, there's something poetic about it — for 500 years, ordinary people were forbidden from entering. Now, anyone can walk through those gates. And the wooden structures? They're built without a single nail — all fitted together by clever joinery. And they're still standing after centuries. That's pretty impressive if you ask me.
